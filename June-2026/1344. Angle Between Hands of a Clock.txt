1344. Angle Between Hands of a Clock


T(n) = S(n) = O(1)

var angleClock = function(hour, minutes) {
    const hourAngle = hour * 30 + minutes * 0.5;
    const minuteAngle = minutes * 6;

    const diff = Math.abs(hourAngle - minuteAngle);

    return Math.min(diff, 360 - diff);
};


// Approach:
- Think of the clock as a circle of 360°. Since there are 12 hour marks, the angle between consecutive hour numbers is 360 / 12 = 30°. i.e In each hr the hr angle moves 30°.

- Similarly, since there are 60 minute marks, each minute corresponds to 360 / 60 = 6°. 

- The minute hand is straightforward: if the time is minutes, its angle from 12 o'clock is simply minutes × 6. The hour hand is the key insight. At h:00, it would be at h × 30°, but the hour hand does not stay fixed between hours—it continuously moves toward the next number. Since it travels 30° in 60 minutes, it moves 0.5° per minute. Therefore, at h:m, the hour hand's angle is h × 30 + m × 0.5. Once we know the positions of both hands, we compute the absolute difference between their angles. However, two angles are formed between the hands (for example, 75° and 285°), and the problem asks for the smaller one. So the final answer is the minimum of diff and 360 - diff.



Mental model to remember:

* Minute hand → 6° per minute
* Hour hand → 30° per hour + 0.5° per minute
* Answer → smaller angle between the two positions.

## Optimal JS Solution


var angleClock = function(hour, minutes) {
    const hourAngle = (hour % 12) * 30 + minutes * 0.5;
    const minuteAngle = minutes * 6;

    const diff = Math.abs(hourAngle - minuteAngle);

    return Math.min(diff, 360 - diff);
};

## Line-by-line intuition

### 1. Hour hand angle

const hourAngle = (hour % 12) * 30 + minutes * 0.5;

Think of the clock as 360°.

There are 12 hours:

360 / 12 = 30°


So:

1 o'clock = 30°
2 o'clock = 60°
3 o'clock = 90°
...


That's:
(hour % 12) * 30 [%12 bcoz in case hr is in railway i.e till 23]


But the hour hand doesn't stay fixed.

At `3:30`, it has moved halfway toward `4`.

It moves:

30° per hour
30° / 60 minutes
= 0.5° per minute


So we add:


minutes * 0.5


Example:

text
3:30

hourAngle
= 3 * 30 + 30 * 0.5
= 90 + 15
= 105°


---

### 2. Minute hand angle


const minuteAngle = minutes * 6;


The minute hand makes a full circle (360°) in 60 minutes.

text
360 / 60 = 6°


per minute.

Example:

text
30 minutes

30 * 6
= 180°


---

### 3. Difference between hands


const diff = Math.abs(hourAngle - minuteAngle);


Example:

text
hourAngle   = 105°
minuteAngle = 180°

diff = 75°


---

### 4. Smaller angle

There are always **two paths** between the hands.

Example:

text
75°


clockwise, and

text
360 - 75 = 285°


the other way around.

The problem asks for the **smaller** angle.

So:


return Math.min(diff, 360 - diff);

---

### Interview one-liner

The whole problem boils down to remembering:

Hour hand  -> 30° per hour + 0.5° per minute
Minute hand -> 6° per minute


Once you compute those two angles, it's just:


Math.min(
    Math.abs(hourAngle - minuteAngle),
    360 - Math.abs(hourAngle - minuteAngle)
);
